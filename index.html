Physics

Rotational Mechanics

Moment of inertia for a rod rotating about its end:

I = ⅓ML²

For a concentrated mass at distance L:

I = ML²

Drag Force

Aerodynamic drag as a function of speed and velocity vector:

F_drag = -k · speed · v_vec

Where k is the drag coefficient, speed is the magnitude of velocity, and v_vec is the velocity vector direction.

Rigid Body Dynamics

Moment of Inertia on Rotational Axis

For a rod rotating about its end.

Linear-Tangential Velocity Relation

v = ωr

Where v is linear velocity, ω (omega) is angular velocity, and r is radius.

Torque-Angular Acceleration Relation

τ = Iα

Where τ (tau) is torque, I is moment of inertia, and α (alpha) is angular acceleration.

Parallel Axis Theorem

I = I_cm + Md²

Where I_cm is the moment of inertia about the center of mass, M is mass, and d is the distance from the center of mass to the new axis.

Aerodynamics

Drag Coefficient

k = (p · C_d · A) / (2m)

Where p is air density, C_d is the drag coefficient, A is cross-sectional area, and m is mass.

Business Analytics

Cohort Analytics

Retention Rate (Day N)

Rₙ = (Active Players on Day N / Total Users who joined on Day 0) × 100

Measures what percentage of users return on a specific day after joining.

Churn Rate

C = 100% - R₁

The percentage of users who don't return after Day 1. R₁ is Day 1 retention.

Lifetime Value (LTV)

LTV = Daily ARPPU / Daily Churn Rate

Where ARPPU is Average Revenue Per Paying User.
